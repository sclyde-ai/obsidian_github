- weight
    $$
    w = (w_1, w_2, ...,w_n)^\top
    $$
    $$
    \sum_{i=1}^n w_i = 1
    $$
- expected return
    $$
    \mu_p = \mathbb E[R_w] 
    = \mathbb E[\sum_{i=1}^n w_i R_i] 
    = \sum_{i=1}^n w_i \mathbb E[R_i] 
    = \sum_{i=1}^n w_i \mathbb \mu_i
    $$
- variance
    $$
    \sigma_p^2 = w^\top \Sigma w
    $$
